5 Distinct Residues Sums

Lemma 9 Lemma 3.1
#

Let \(c_{0},\ldots ,c_{k}\) be nonnegative integers and suppose \(\sum _{i=0}^{k}c_{i}=m+\binom {k+1}{2}\), where \(m\) is a nonnegative integer. Then the coefficient of \(\prod _{i=0}^{k}x_{i}^{c_{i}}\) in the polynomial

\[ (x_{0}+x_{1}+\ldots +x_{k})^{m}\prod _{k\geq i{\gt}j\geq 0}(x_{i}-x_{j}) \]

is

\[ \frac{m!}{c_{0}!c_{1}!\ldots c_{k}!}\prod _{k\geq i{\gt}j\geq 0}(c_{i}-c_{j}). \]

The product \(\prod _{k\geq i{\gt}j\geq 0}(x_{i}-x_{j})\) is the Vandermonde determinant \(\det (x_{i}^{j})_{0\leq i\leq k,0\leq j\leq k}\). The result follows by combinatorial manipulation.

Theorem 10 Proposition 1.2 Proof
#

Define \(h(x_{0},\ldots ,x_{k})=\prod _{k\geq i{\gt}j\geq 0}(x_{i}-x_{j})\). Suppose \(|A_{i}|=c_{i}+1\) and put \(m=\sum _{i=0}^{k}c_{i}-\binom {k+1}{2}\). By Lemma 3.1 the coefficient of \(\prod _{i=0}^{k}x_{i}^{c_{i}}\) in \(h\cdot (x_{0}+\ldots +x_{k})^{m}\) is

\[ \frac{m!}{c_{0}!c_{1}!\ldots c_{k}!}\prod _{k\geq i{\gt}j\geq 0}(c_{i}-c_{j}), \]

which is nonzero modulo \(p\) since \(m{\lt}p\) and the \(c_{i}\) are pairwise distinct. Theorem 2.1 gives the result.
