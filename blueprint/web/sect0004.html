4 Cauchy-Davenport Theorem Proof

Theorem 8 Cauchy-Davenport Theorem Proof
#

If \(|A|+|B|\leq p+1\) apply Theorem 2.1 with \(h\equiv 1\), \(k=1\), \(A_{0}=A\), \(A_{1}=B\) and \(m=|A|+|B|-2\). Here \(c_{0}=|A|-1\), \(c_{1}=|B|-1\) and the relevant coefficient is \(\binom {m}{c_{0}}\) which is nonzero modulo \(p\) (as \(m{\lt}p\)). If \(|A|+|B|{\gt}p+1\) replace \(B\) by a subset \(B^{\prime }\) of cardinality \(p+1-|A|\) and apply the result above to conclude \(|A+B|\geq p\).
